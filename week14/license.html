Sailplane Certificate Eligibility Requirements

In the United States, licensing of aircraft pilots is under the authority of the FAA. Additional sailplane training information may be found on the SSA web site.

These are the requirements for flying a sailplane solo:

  • An individual must be at least 14 years old.
  • A student must be tested to demonstrate satisfactory aeronautical knowledge.
  • A student must have received and logged ground and flight training.
  • A student pilot must demonstrate satisfactory proficiency and safety.

To be eligible for a private pilot certificate with a sailplane rating:

  • An individual must be at least 16 years old.
  • A student must complete the training and flight time requirements.
  • A student must pass a knowledge test.
  • A student must successfully complete a practical test.

Note: Locate a qualified sailplane ground school via the SSA web site.
