Getting Into The Air

Tow Plane:

The most common means of getting aloft in the USA is to get towed by a powered aircraft up to a predetermined release altitude (generally 1,000-2,000ft).

Winch Launch:

This technique is very popular in Europe and Asia. In this case, the sailplane is connected via cable to a motorized winch at the far end of the runway. The pilot releases the cable when directly over the winch.

Self-Launch:

Some much more expensive sailplanes have built-in engines that are powerful enough to self-launch the sailplane. Once aloft, the engine is either "feathered" or collapsed into the body of the aircraft.
