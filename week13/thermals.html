Types of Rising Air

Sailplane pilots generally seek three types of rising air: thermals, ridge lift, and mountain wave.

Thermals:

Generated by hot air rising on the surface rising into the atmosphere. Thermals can rise thousands of feet into the air.

Ridge Lift:

Wind can blow against a mountain or ridge line and the air flow is deflected upward

Mountain Wave:

Mountain waves are caused by strong winds crossing a mountain range.
